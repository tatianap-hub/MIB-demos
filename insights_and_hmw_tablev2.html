1Rental is not perceived as a daily wardrobe habit, but as a solution for specific moments.Mentioned by 10 out of 11 users“Renting — rather for special occasions. Not for everyday clothes.” — Veronique
“For events like weddings or corporate events, it’s absolutely yes, it’s super.” — Daria
“Buying doesn’t make sense — especially if it’s a serious dress, you wear it once.” — Oxana
“For everyday wear, I probably wouldn’t rent clothes. But for special occasions, yes.” — Mayya
“Either I have a specific event for which I need an outfit, like a wedding.” — Constance		1How might we position peer-to-peer rental as a simple, desirable and safe way to access exceptional pieces for specific life moments?
2Trust is the entry condition: without visible safeguards, users hesitate to rent or lend.Mentioned by 10 out of 11 users“There needs to be an active, experienced account — a clear profile. That gives a sense of security.” — Veronique
“I check everything very carefully. All the reviews, ideally with photos.” — Nelly
“The platform should protect my interests. Compensation for dry cleaning, repairs, and so on. Also a deposit.” — Daria
“I would want to see their profile, reviews, and understand why they need the item.” — Mayya
“I’d want to have the person’s profile and reassurance on hygiene.” — Audrey		2How might we design a visible trust system that reassures both renters and lenders before, during and after each rental?
3The main barrier for lenders is not only risk, but the operational effort required.Mentioned by 8 out of 11 users“You have to take photos, upload, pack, send. For me, it is more of a constraint.” — Veronique
“Taking nice photos, posting them, moderation takes a very long time.” — Alia
“When you have many things you want to sell or list, you often do not get around to it. You need to take photos and retouch them; it takes time.” — Klavdia
“The main barrier, it’s mostly the time it takes. And honestly I don’t want to dedicate time to that.” — Constance
“Sending items requires effort, and I might be away.” — Capitoline		3How might we reduce the effort of lending through assisted listing, AI-generated photos, integrated cleaning, delivery support, storage or concierge-style services?
4Clothing is emotionally and personally sensitive, so users need control over what is shareable.Mentioned by 7 out of 11 users“These are items I earned myself, something I wanted for a long time, and they carry memories.” — Klavdia
“My clothes are quite distinctive. If someone wears them, people will say we have two identical coats. I do not want that.” — Alia
“I keep things for quite a long time before deciding to get rid of them. It is hard for me to part with my clothes.” — Nelly
“Only things that I no longer wear. I wouldn’t want to take risks.” — Daria
“Shoes — no. Shoes are more personal.” — Veronique		4How might we help users identify which items feel safe to share without threatening their attachment, identity or sense of control?
5Community can create value, but only if it is useful, local and low-effort.Mentioned by 8 out of 11 users“I do not see it as a community. It is more of a service — you go in, find something, and take it.” — Veronique
“Having a community and it being in your neighborhood would be a factor of security for me.” — Constance
“You can see what new clothes have appeared near you, for example within a 500-meter radius.” — Klavdia
“For me it would be something new, but generally I would say — all new communities attract.” — Oxana		5How might we build a community that feels useful rather than demanding — based on proximity, style compatibility, inspiration and trust?
